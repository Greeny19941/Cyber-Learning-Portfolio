# CIA Triad – TryHackMe Lab Notes

## Overview

The CIA Triad is a fundamental concept in cybersecurity, representing three core principles that underpin system and information security:

- Confidentiality  
- Integrity  
- Availability  

This lab served as a gentle refresher of these core security pillars and their practical application in securing systems.

---

## Confidentiality

Confidentiality ensures that only authorised individuals or groups are able to access or modify data.

Key mechanisms that support confidentiality include:

- Encryption
- Access control systems
- Authentication and authorisation processes

The goal is to prevent unauthorised disclosure of sensitive information.

---

## Integrity

Integrity ensures that data cannot be modified by unauthorised individuals.

If integrity is compromised:

- Data may be altered without permission
- Information can no longer be trusted
- This can lead to serious operational, financial, or safety consequences

Maintaining integrity ensures data accuracy and trustworthiness over time.

---

## Availability

Availability ensures that systems, services, and data are accessible when needed.

Key considerations include:

- System uptime and reliability
- Redundancy and failover systems
- Protection against denial-of-service attacks

If availability is lost, it can have severe impacts—especially in critical environments such as healthcare, where time-sensitive access to records is essential.

---

## Practical Lab Summary

The practical exercise involved categorising 9 different scenarios into the correct CIA pillar.

This was a drag-and-drop activity designed to reinforce understanding of:

- How real-world examples map to each pillar
- The importance of distinguishing between confidentiality, integrity, and availability impacts

The exercise was straightforward and served as a useful reinforcement of concepts already covered in previous studies, including ISC2 and CompTIA coursework.

---

## Key Takeaway

The CIA Triad remains one of the most important foundational models in cybersecurity. Understanding how each pillar applies in real-world scenarios is essential for both exams and practical security work.
