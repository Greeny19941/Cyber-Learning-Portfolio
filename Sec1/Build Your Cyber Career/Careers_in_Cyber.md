# Careers in Cyber

This was a very basic lab introducing the different career paths within cybersecurity, and highlighting the growing global demand for cybersecurity professionals. It also reinforced the idea that cybersecurity is a field of **continuous learning**, something I hope to remain consistent with throughout my career.

The lab explored several roles in more detail, starting with the **Security Analyst**. This is a role I believe would suit me well, likely more than being on the "red team". However, understanding red team techniques would clearly help strengthen blue team activities and defensive thinking.

Incident response particularly appeals to me, as I tend to be quite **methodical and structured in the way I think**, and I enjoy investigating problems in depth and going down technical rabbit holes when necessary.

Cybersecurity is also a broad entry point, so I imagine that if I am given the opportunity to move into the field, I may eventually find a **more specialised niche** where I can develop deeper expertise.

Another role that appeals to me is **Security Engineer**, especially given my previous experience working with system infrastructure at Netbuilder.

While **penetration testing** does not feel like my main area of interest at this stage, I am still keen to develop skills in this area to build a broader understanding of cybersecurity. There are clear overlaps between offensive and defensive security, and having awareness of attacker techniques can only strengthen defensive capabilities.

## Reflection

This lab helped clarify the range of roles within cybersecurity. At this stage, defensive roles such as Security Analyst or Incident Response feel like a natural fit given my mindset and previous infrastructure experience.
