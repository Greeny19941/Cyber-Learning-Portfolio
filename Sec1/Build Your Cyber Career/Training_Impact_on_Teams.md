# Training Impact on Teams

This was more of a “soft skills” lab, highlighting the importance of cybersecurity training within organisations.

A key point was that it is far better for teams to train in **sandbox or simulated environments** rather than learning during a real incident. Proper training reduces the likelihood of security incidents and helps teams respond more effectively when they do occur.

Training also increases an organisation’s **capability and resilience** without necessarily needing to hire additional staff. A well-prepared team can handle incidents much more efficiently, whereas teams that are forced to learn during an active incident may struggle to resolve issues quickly enough.

The lab also discussed the **financial aspect of training**. Some organisations actively encourage training, while others require employees to make a business case to justify the investment.

When organisations choose to use external training providers, selecting the right vendor is important. Considerations include:

- Experience delivering training to other organisations
- Whether the training platform is accessible and scalable for employees
- The quality and relevance of the content provided

Although this was not a highly technical lab, it reinforced the importance of **continuous learning**, which is especially critical in the cybersecurity field.
